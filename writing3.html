Writing 3: Slow/Hard Way


When do you cherish the slow or hard way of doing something? Why? When do you embrace constraints or prefer inefficiency? When do you willingly sacrifice convenience or comfort? What does the limited, slower, or more effortful approach offer that the quicker and easier way does not? What can we learn from this about human fulfillment and the kinds of technologies that might support it?

Making presentations or slide decks. While there are plenty of ways to speed up this process such as using pre-made templates, Ai tools to beautify slides, or simply pasting text into bullet points, I find that I get more out of the process when I take the time to create the slides myself. I like to choose the colors, fonts, and layout that I think best fit the content. I also think it's important to figure out how to present information wihtout overwhelming the audience. It's about shaping a clear point and telling a compelling story or narrative, not just displaying information. I enjoy the challenge of figuring out the most visually appealing and convincing way to communicate ideas in a way that feels engaging and easy to follow. Taking this more effortful, hands-on approach also helps me understand the material more deeply, and it makes the final product feel more rewarding when everything comes together.
