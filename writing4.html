Writing 4: Appreciate/Long-For


What is something you appreciate or long for from the past? Why? It could be something you lived through, or something from the distant past. It might be a tradition or an antiquated value that you appreciate, an old way of doing things that you prefer / are fond of, or even a personal time or cultural moment that you hope to reconnect with. What do you appreciate about it? What wisdom does it contain? What can it teach us about living well today?

Something I appreciate and long for from the past is my childhood. I miss living in the same house as my family and growing up with my older brother. During those years, we spent a lot of time together, whether it was eating dinner as a family, watching TV, playing board games, or just being around each other. While we still see each other every year during the holidays, there was something especially magical about moments like birthdays, Christmas, and other celebrations when I was younger. Throwing fun bday parties, decorating the house, putting up Christmas lights, and the excitement of Santa made everything feel full of anticipation and joy. Now, our celebrations are much simpler as we usually go out to eat and catch up on what is going on in our lives. Childhood felt like a simpler time in some ways because it was easier to be fully present and live in the moment. Now, it often feels like there is always something to worry about, whether it is school, career decisions, or what comes next. Looking back, I appreciate those childhood moments along with all the joy and excitement they brought.
