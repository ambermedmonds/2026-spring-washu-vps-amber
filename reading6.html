The Coming Age of Calm Technology

by Mark Weiser and John Seely Brown

I thought the concept of “calm technology” — the idea that we should design technology to support our lives in a way that keeps us feeling calm and in control — was really thought-provoking. It was especially interesting to think about how our relationship with technology has evolved over time, from shared computers to personal computers to now having it embedded everywhere in everyday life. Nowadays, I feel like I always have some sort of device attached to me, whether it's my phone or my computer. Many of these products seem designed to constantly capture my attention through notifications, alerts, and messages, and I often find myself getting distracted easily as my attention is pulled in different directions.

I also found it fascinating to read about how calm technology should move between the center and periphery of our attention, informing us without interrupting. Instead of always trying to capture our focus, there's an opportunity for technology to exist more in the background by providing useful context and environmental clues without making us feel overwhelmed. This makes me curious about what future products might look like if they were intentionally designed this way, and how our relationship with technology might change moving forward.
