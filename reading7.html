Do Artifacts Have Ethics?

by L. M. Sacasas

I think this article raises a really interesting question about whether artifacts have ethics built into them. This feels especially relevant right now with the rise of AI, where there are so many ethical concerns around fake content, trust, safety, and identity, etc. It's easy to think of these as just issues of how people use the technology, but Sacasas pushes us to think more deeply than that.

As technology continues to advance with AI, robots, and other new tools, it becomes increasingly important to think intentionally about the impact these tools have on society. We should consider not just what they allow us to do, but how they shape behavior, relationships, and even the way we see the world. I liked the example of the hammer because it shows that tools can actually influence how users think and feel, guiding both perception and action. Overall, the article made me reflect on what kind of world technology is creating, and whether we are being thoughtful enough about the long-term effects of the tools we are building.
