Writing 1: Routine/Practice


What is a ritual, practice, or routine in your life that is important for your psychological wellbeing and/or fulfillment? Why? How does this ritual or behavior contribute to your life? What aspect of it makes it valuable to you? What can it teach us about the nature or ingredients of human fulfillment more broadly?

One practice or routine in my life that is important for my psychological wellbeing and fulfillment is exercising. As a student athlete on the tennis team, I like the structure of having a practice/training schedule because it helps me be more productive and focused throughout the day. I find that when I don't have a set routine in place, I tend to get distracted or procrastinate more easily.

Exercise also serves as an important mental outlet for me. When I feel stressed, overwhelmed, or mentally drained, physical activity can be a great way for me to reset and take my mind off things. It helps me release stress in a healthy way and often leaves me feeling more energized afterward.

Beyond the physical and mental benefits, one thing I value most about exercise is the social aspect. Some of my closest friendships have come from sports, and being part of a team makes has been one of the most fun and rewarding parts of my college experience. Training and competing with teammates has made everything more enjoyable and motivating because we can support each other, push one another to improve, and celebrate our successes together.

What makes this practice or routine so valuable to me is that it brings together several things people naturally look for: structure, personal growth, a mental outlet, and connection with others. More broadly, I think it shows that fulfillment often comes from activities that challenge us, give us purpose, and make us feel part of something bigger than ourselves.
